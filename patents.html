    Patents

    A fundamental building block of Intellectual Property Rights (IPRs)

    Newly developed products, services, processes or technology involve a huge investment of resources such as effort, money, etc. It is important to prevent others from misusing your product, service, idea or process, etc. The Intellectual Property Rights Law protects innovations and inventions via patents. Patents accord the rightful proprietorship to the owner for the invention/innovation by defining the patent claims. A single patent can cover combinations of different definitions of related inventions. To obtain rights to a patent, you have to follow a stringent legal process and get the documentation right. It is vital that the patent specification and phrasing of claims in the legal documentation are accurate so that you will have valuable and defendable rights to the patent. This can be done by a combination of strong technical acumen and a deep understanding of patent law.
